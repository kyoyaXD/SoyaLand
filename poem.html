Maiden I Cherish

In the wintery month of December,
When all things are frigid and bleak,
A charming maiden stands with her arms widespread,
Within whom refuge I seek.

Her love infuses me with warmth and power,
Her majestic gaze enough to thaw the Arctic ice,
Though misunderstandings between us stand tall as a tower,
Our companionship breaks through, ending in something forever nice.

The frosty weather, to which many a hearts succumb,
An eternal flame does our love ignite,
The twin cores of youthful vigour battling the numb,
Withstanding the inevitable frostbite.

Under the pale weak sun, blooms a scarlet chrysanthemum,
Against all odds, with the will to flourish,
For destiny is naught but bogus scum,
When I am with this maiden I cherish.
